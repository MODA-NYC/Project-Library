There are two steps involved in the establishment of pay bands, the only derived attribute derived by MODA in the Table:

  • First, employees in the same employee status but whose pay is calculated based on different frequencies1, such as daily, hourly, annual or prorated, need to have their pay annualized or converted for pay comparison within the same employee status and;
  • Second, once the pay is standardized, a common pay band is assigned across the full-time, part-time and seasonal employees to aggregate employees.

2.3.1. Pay Annualization and Conversion (FYI, Steve - this will be a sub-sub-heading to be linked with the left pane for 2.3 above)

To support the establishment of pay bands, MODA first standardized the pay of 9,136 employees (approximately 5%) out of the total 181,136 employees, due to the differences in the pay calculation frequency of the full-time (annual, daily, and hourly) and part-time (hourly and prorated annual) employees, by:

  • Annualizing the pay of 7,961 full-time employees whose pay is calculated based on a daily or hourly frequency using the conversion rate of 261 days/year or 1,827 hours/year, respectively, for the majority of these employees and;
  • Converting the salaries of 1,175 part-time employees to an hourly pay who are paid on prorated annual2 basis using the conversion rate of 1,958 hours/year for the majority of these employees.

For example, a full-time employee in the civil service title of Carpenter (EEO-4 Job Category: Skilled Craft) whose pay as of 12/31/2018 was $349.16/day, had their pay annualized using the rate of 261 days/yr (Appendix E)3, which amounts to a salary of $91,130.80/yr. Similarly, a part-time employee in the civil service title of School Lunch Aide (EEO-4 Job Category: Service and Maintenance) who was paid $29,539 on a prorated annual basis, had their prorated salary converted into its hourly equivalent using the calculation rate of 1,958 hrs/year (Appendix E), which amounts to a pay of $15.10/hr.

MODA’s approach to standardize pay, with the full list of annualization and conversion rates in Appendix E, is consistent with City methodology; however there exists no standard Citywide methodology to standardize pay for comparison.

Out of the 9,136 employees whose pay was standardized, MODA identified 79 part-time employees paid on a prorated annual basis whose work duration information was unavailable. The prorated annual salaries for these employees correspond to a certain work duration during 2018, which does not exist in NYCAPS. As a result, MODA used the standard number of hours worked per year in these titles to convert the prorated salaries of these employees into their hourly rates.
